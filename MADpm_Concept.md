# MADpm – Make a Difference, Problem Master

## 1. Executive Summary
MADpm is a community-driven platform designed to systematically capture, discuss, qualify, and solve real-world problems across domains. It brings together people from diverse backgrounds—product management, engineering, design, marketing, operations, arts, commerce, and students—who share one common trait: a passion for observing problems and solving them thoughtfully.

Unlike existing idea boards, forums, or social platforms that focus on solutions, opinions, or outcomes, MADpm is intentionally problem-first. It creates a structured repository of high-quality problem statements, enriched through focused discussions, domain expertise, validation signals, and collaborative problem-solving.

The platform aims to reduce blind bets, shallow ideation, and poorly validated solutions by enabling disciplined problem discovery and community-led refinement—while simultaneously creating learning, career, and innovation opportunities.

## 2. Why MADpm Is Needed

### 2.1 The Current State (Reality)
* Most products fail not because of poor execution, but because they solve the wrong problem or a weak version of it.
* Problem discovery today is:
  * Informal and scattered (Twitter threads, Slack groups, Notion docs, Reddit, LinkedIn posts)
  * Biased by individual experience, seniority, or loud opinions
  * Lacking structure, comparability, and traceability
* Students and early professionals struggle to demonstrate problem-thinking skills—only outcomes.
* Founders and teams rely heavily on:
  * Assumptions
  * Anecdotal feedback
  * Expensive research or closed user panels

### 2.2 The Gap
There is no neutral, domain-agnostic platform that:
* Treats problems as first-class citizens
* Encourages structured observation before solutioning
* Allows diverse minds to collaborate meaningfully
* Creates measurable proof of problem-solving capability

MADpm exists to fill this gap.

## 8. Platform Structure (Core Components)

### 8.1 Problem Submission Framework
Each problem includes:
* Observation context
* Personal experience / trigger
* Affected users
* Frequency & severity
* Impact (time, money, emotion)
* Existing alternatives / workarounds
* Open questions

### 8.2 Community Signals
* Upvote / downvote relevance
* Comment-based validation or invalidation
* "Already solved" tagging
* Domain expert annotations

### 8.3 Focused Problem Rooms
* Opt-in deep discussions
* Cross-functional participation
* Research, hypothesis, and insights

### 8.4 Solution Exploration (Optional, Secondary)
* Brainstorming spaces
* Prototypes, POCs, experiments
* Learnings documented

### 8.5 Reputation & MADpm Score
* Quality of problems posted
* Depth of participation
* Peer and expert endorsements
